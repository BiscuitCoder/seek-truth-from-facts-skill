# On Contradiction — Analytical Tool

The central thesis of On Contradiction: **the development of things is driven by their internal contradictions**. Analyzing any problem is, at its core, analyzing its contradiction structure.

## Universality and Particularity of Contradiction

**Universality**: Contradiction exists in every process of every thing. Without contradiction there is no development. Do not avoid contradiction in analysis — actively seek it out.

**Particularity**: Every contradiction has its own specific character. Do not apply a universal formula.

Operational points:
- Ask "what is the contradiction in this problem?" before asking "which model should I apply?"
- Find what makes this problem **different from the general case** — that is often the key to the analysis
- The same type of problem may manifest entirely different contradictions at different stages or for different actors

## Principal and Secondary Contradictions

When multiple contradictions coexist, the **principal contradiction** determines the overall trajectory; secondary contradictions are subordinate to it.

**Criteria for identifying the principal contradiction**:
- Resolving it causes other contradictions to loosen or disappear
- It is the root cause from which other problems arise
- When resources are limited, it is the one worth prioritizing

**Common mistakes**:
- Treating the most urgent as the most important (urgent ≠ principal)
- Treating surface phenomena as the root contradiction (e.g., treating "no traffic" as the principal contradiction when the real issue is "no product differentiation")
- Listing many contradictions without ranking them — this is equivalent to no analysis

**Operation**:
1. List all visible contradictions and problems
2. For each, ask: is this a cause or an effect? If resolved, which other problems would be resolved along with it?
3. Rank them and identify the principal contradiction

## The Principal Aspect of a Contradiction

Every contradiction consists of two opposing aspects. The two aspects are not equal in force; the **principal aspect** determines the nature of the contradiction.

Example — analyzing "can a startup succeed?":
- Favorable aspect: market demand exists, team execution is strong
- Unfavorable aspect: only 3 months of runway, competitor already has first-mover advantage
→ If the unfavorable aspect is dominant, the nature of this contradiction leans toward "high failure risk"

**Operation**:
- Define both aspects of the contradiction explicitly
- Determine which is dominant (basis: scale, trend, structural vs. incidental)
- Ground the conclusion in the principal aspect, not a simple balance of both sides

## Identity and Struggle of Contradictions

**Identity**: The two aspects of a contradiction are mutually dependent and interpenetrating; under certain conditions they can transform into each other.
**Struggle**: The two aspects mutually exclude and oppose each other.

Analytical value:
- **Find transformation conditions**: Under what conditions can the currently infeasible become feasible?
- **Identify dependency relations**: Opposing aspects often presuppose each other (e.g., supply and demand)
- **Anticipate turning points**: When the balance of forces between the principal aspects shifts, the nature of the contradiction will flip

---

## Conditions for Contradiction Transformation

Contradictions are not static. When conditions change, the nature and ranking of contradictions transform.

Questions to answer during analysis:
1. Under what conditions will the current principal contradiction transform into a secondary one?
2. What conditions must change for something currently infeasible to become feasible?
3. Which conditions can be actively created, and which require waiting for external change?

## Operational Guide

When using On Contradiction to analyze a problem, think in this sequence:

```
1. What contradictions exist in this problem? (enumerate)
2. Which is the principal contradiction? (rank + rationale)
3. What are the two aspects of the principal contradiction? Which is dominant? (determine nature)
4. What is the particularity of this contradiction? (avoid generic frameworks)
5. Under what conditions can the contradiction transform? (find the path)
```

When presenting output: **state the principal contradiction judgment first, then expand the analysis**. Do not list five parallel problems and conclude with "comprehensive consideration is needed."
